Voucher System

Students and parents make this club a community of mutual support and learning by helping with a wide variety of tasks. Our voucher system offers other ways to contribute. You will need vouchers to grade and can use them to pay for extra training or to reduce future training fees.

Our voucher system is for service to aikidō generally, and Aikidō Nelson specifically. It is based on the Aikidō Nelson values of support, integrity, and celebration.

It also identifies and makes transparent the jobs needing to be done for the dōjō to run well.

The system works like this: All members of the dojo and/or their families give some tangible level of service to the Aikidō Nelson community during the course of each calendar year and get voucher credits. Specific numbers of vouchers are needed for each grading.

Earning vouchers

There are many easy ways to earn vouchers, as we want to make sure everyone can get enough to grade. Each type of service/task/job that is completed is rewarded with varying amounts of vouchers (in brackets).

Tasks include participating in (1–2 vouchers) or organising (2–5) meetings, maintenance, cleaning, promotion, demonstrations and social events; providing flowers (2); raising funds (6–60); maintaining (5–15) books, database, website, dogi lease system or voucher log; and holding a formal club position like chair, treasurer, secretary etc. (15–25). Please talk to the Voucher Manager or the Dojo Cho to find out how you can help.

Spending vouchers

After the first grading, each grade requires the following number of vouchers:

  • 0 vouchers are required for 13th to 11th Kyu (and for the first grading)
  • 2 vouchers are required for Semi-10th to Semi-8th Kyu
  • 5 vouchers are required for 8th to 1st Kyu

Vouchers not used for gradings are redeemable at the end of each calendar year for next year’s fees (i.e. you can claim a credit of $10 per voucher for the following calendar year (January–December).

Vouchers expire at the end of each annual redemption period.

Vouchers accumulated in excess of fees owed for a given year have no cash value and cannot be redeemed.

Grades and belts

Grading is not compulsory but it’s a good way to mark your progress.

Adult gradings are held twice a year. Juniors have the chance to take gradings once each term.

To be eligible for your next grade you must complete the minimum number of training days and know the syllabus.
